Banarasi silk is a fine silk saree originating from Varanasi, known for gold and silver brocade work.

Kanchipuram silk sarees are made in Tamil Nadu and are famous for their durability and vibrant colors.

Pashmina wool comes from Kashmir and is made from the soft hair of Changthangi goats.

Khadi is a handspun and handwoven fabric promoted by Mahatma Gandhi during India's freedom movement.

Cotton textiles are widely produced in India and are breathable and suitable for hot climates.

Ikat is a dyeing technique used to create patterns on fabric before weaving.

Bandhani is a tie-dye textile art from Gujarat and Rajasthan.

Chanderi fabric from Madhya Pradesh is known for its lightweight and glossy texture.
